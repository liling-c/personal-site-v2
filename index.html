About Me.

Welcome to my website. I'm a recent graduate from Boston University who somehow stumbled her way into becoming a programmer. As a kid, I spent my time drawing and folding origami. I told my parents that I wanted to be an artist when I grew up; my interest in programming began with a happy accident. In 10th grade, I was placed into a programming class instead of the drawing elective I requested. I wondered, "What is programming?" I attended it and haven't looked back. I look forward to what new things I'll gain each day and hope to acquire the necessary skills to combine all my interests into one project.

Today, I'm a recent graduate of Boston University with a B.A. in Computer Science and a Teaching Assistant (TA) for Girls Who Code's Summer Immersion Program. In my spare time, when I'm not staring at a code editor, I enjoy drawing (read: staring at a blank piece of paper) and hoarding stationery.
